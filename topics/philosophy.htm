Precept philosopy

 

 

 

 

When generating forecast, events can affects production / injection at every time step, such as:

- introduction of a new profiles due to drilling new wells

- conversions of existing wells

- changes in process constraints due to facility upgrades

- shutdowns

 

The date of such events can be "hard":

- Start date of new profile can be manually entered in the "New profiles" section (see New profile)

- Conversion date can be manually entered in the "Conversion" section (see Well conversion)

- Date of changes in constraints can be entered manually in the "Constaints changes vs time" section (see Constraints changes vs time)

- Shutdowns start and end date can be manually entered in the "Shutdowns" section (see Shutdowns)

 

Entering "hard" date does not allow to generate what-if scenario easily .

 

Example

On an offshore field, it is planned to drill 6 wells on 2 different platforms, with Rig move and shutdown associated.

 

Sub-Optimum modeling: using Hard date only.

The above scenario can be modeled using "Hard" date only

Start date of new profiles from the new drilled wells can be entered in the "New profiles" section (see New profiles)

Start and End date of the shutdowns associated with the Rig move can be entered in the "Shutdowns" section (see  Shutdowns)

 

Now let's suppose the drilling schedule has changed, User will then has to changes manually all the new profiles starting date and shutdowns date, which is time consuming and prone to error.

 

 

Optimum modeling: Create Opportunity and place them in the sequence of events

An opportunity should be created for every new well to be drilled.

An opportunity should also be created for every Rig Move, with Shutdowns associated

 

A sequence of events needs to be created for the "Rig 1" , (see Sequence of events)

The drilling of 6 wells + 4 Rig moves will then be placed in the sequence of events in the right order.

 

 

Now, if a change in the drilling schedule occurs, such as late arrival for the Rig, only the mobilization date of the Rig has to be changed ,and the sequence of events and associated shutdowns will automatically be adjusted.

 

 

A sequence of events can be:

 - drilling of new wells

 - facilities maintenance performed by a maintenance crew

 - Well head maintenance performed by a Well Services crew

 - Stimulation of several wells performed by Stimulation crew
