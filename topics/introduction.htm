Introduction

 

 

 

 

This document serves as Technical reference for Precept structure, installation and maintenance.

This document also serves as User Manual.

 

Precept is a visual development planning system that helps oil and gas companies to model complex projects quickly and accurately – allowing planners and decision makers time to focus on ideas and strategy rather than on the modeling.

 

By making it easy to modify rig schedules, well forecasts, facility limits or planned shutdown, Precept enables you to quickly evaluate alternative development options and therefore optimize production from your assets.

 

 

At every time step in the forecast, wells production and injection potentials are calculated from decline curve analysis (Oil/water/Gas vs Cum or vs time). Using a linear simplex solver, Precept then calculates the effects of constraints  n the network. The generated forecast can then be used for long-term forecasting (field development planning, reserves evaluations) or short term forecast, provided all informations needed (shutdown, uptime, etc) is supplied.

 

Using a linear solver means the network is solved and optimized for rates only, and all rates in the production network are AT STANDARD CONDITIONS

 

For more advanced calculations where network needs to be solved for rates and pressures, or gas-lift optimized at every timestep, Precept can be used as Pre-Post processor for GAP.

 

To generate a hydrocarbons forecast with Precept, three mains inputs are needed:

 - Wells production and injection potentials

 - sequence of events (Drilling schedule, shutdowns, uptime, conversion of wells, etc

 - production networks and its constraints.

 

 

 

After a forecast is generated by Precept, outputs can be visualized in 3 differents ways:

 

- charts: at every node of the network

- timeline: powerful to QC which wells are online/offline and identify production network bottlenecks

- polygon maps
