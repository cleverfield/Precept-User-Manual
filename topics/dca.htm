Decline curves: Production and Injection potentials

 

 

 

 

User can access the DCA module by clicking on the "Decline Curves" icon from Precept main User Interface.

 

The declines Curves Analysis (DCA) module is a powerful module from which Users can define individual Well Potentials. During forecast, shutdown and constraints are applied to Well potentials to generate constrained forecast (the difference being shortfalls).

 

 

At opening, User needs to select the Project / Scenario and the Well that requires DCA generation.

Analogue wells can be plotted in the background to be used as guidelines.

 

 

5 types of DCA methods can be used to generate Well Potentials:

 - ARPS exponential

 - ARPS Hyperbolic

 - ARPS Harmonic

 - "Custom vs Cum"

 - "Custom vs Time"

 

 

ARPS DCA:

Detailed information about ARPS DCA can be found in the following link: https://petrowiki.spe.org/Production_forecasting_decline_curve_analysis

 

 

Custom Vs Cum

In the "Custom vs Com" method:

 - for Oil producer, Well potential is generated by modifying Liquid rate, BSW and GOR. Within one segment, Liquid rate, BSW and GOR are a modeled as a linear function of Cumulative Oil

 - for Gas producer, Well potential is generated by modifying Gas rate, Water-Gas-Ratio (WGR) and Condensate-Gas-Ratio (CGR).  Within one segment, Gas rate, Water-Gas-Ratio (WGR) and Condensate-Gas-Ratio (CGR)are a modeled as a linear function of Cumulative Gas

 - for Water Injector, Well potential is generated by modifying Water Injection rate. Within one segment, Water Injection rate is modeled as a linear function of Cumulative Water Injection.

 - for Gas Injector, Well potential is generated by modifying Gas Injection rate. Within one segment, Gas Injection rate is modeled as a linear function of Cumulative Gas Injection.

 

Since potentials are function of cumulative production/injection, when a shutdown occurs while generating constrained forecast, the well will restart at the same rate as before the shutdown.

 

 

 

Custom Vs Time

In the "Custom vs Time" method:

 - for Oil producer, Well potential is generated by modifying Liquid rate, BSW and GOR. Within one segment, Liquid rate, BSW and GOR are a modeled as a linear function of Time

 - for Gas producer, Well potential is generated by modifying Gas rate, Water-Gas-Ratio (WGR) and Condensate-Gas-Ratio (CGR).  Within one segment, Gas rate, Water-Gas-Ratio (WGR) and Condensate-Gas-Ratio (CGR)are a modeled as a linear function of Time

 - for Water Injector, Well potential is generated by modifying Water Injection rate. Within one segment, Water Injection rate is modeled as a linear function of Cumulative Water Injection.

 - for Gas Injector, Well potential is generated by modifying Gas Injection rate. Within one segment, Gas Injection rate is modeled as a linear function of Cumulative Gas Injection.

 

When a shutdown occurs while generating constrained forecast, the well will restart at the rate given by the DCA at that specific restart time (the well potential keeps on declining even it is shut-in)

 

 

"Custom vs Cum" and "Custom vs Time" during shutdown

 

 

 

The view DCA "Vs Time" or 'vs Cumulative" is only a visualization option. That means that when "Custom Vs Cum" method is selected, Users can visualize the DCA as a function of time, but are not able to modify it.

Similarly, when "Custom Vs Time" method is selected, Users can visualize the DCA as a function of Cumulative, but are not able to modify it.

 

 

Modify Decline Curves

Modifying decline curves can be performed using 2 methods:

 - change manually the value in the Potential table

 - drag and drop the "dots' on the chart (for "Custom vs Time" and "Custom Vs Cum", segment can be added or removed using the + and - button)

 

 

Low, Mid and High Case

For every well, Low, Mid and High case can be generated. The Low, Mid and High Case are used when generating probabilistic forecast.
