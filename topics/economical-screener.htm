Economical screener

 

 

 

 

Precept has not been developed as a Economical software for operating Oil and Gas Assets.

However, Precept has a basic Economical screener.

 

CAPEX

Capex cost can be entered when:

- a new profiles in put online or a converison occurs

- a change in constraints occurs, as a result of Field debottlenecking or commisisoning of new facilities

OPEX

Opex inputs can be entered by clicking on the "OPEX" icon in Precept Main User Interface.

 

In Precept, OPEX are calculated a a function of fluid treated.

In the example below:

A cost of 10,000 USD/d is introduced when the Node A whether the node A is up and running or not.

A cost of 3 USD/barrel of liquid flowing through node A is introduced.

 

A cost of 11 USD/barrel of liquid flowing through node B is introduced.

A cost of 2 USD/mscf/d of gas flowing through node "B_compr" is introduced.

etc..

 

 

 

OPEX cost can be grouped using the "Group Opex to Node" column.

Sales:

To calculate the revenue from from Oil and Gas sales, a Node calles "Sales" need to be introduced in the production network (see Network modelling). The volume of hydrocarbons sold is usually different from the volume produced since some hydrocarbons may be flared or used for power generation.

 

In the example below, the company do not generate any profits from Gas sales. Therefore the reporting Node "Sales" is only connected to the "oil export" node. Note also the doted line between the "Oil export" and "Sales" node, indicating this is not a physical pipeline but a pipeline used for reporting purposes.

 

 

The price of oil and gas at start of forecast and % yearly increase can be input from the 'Settings" form.

 

 

 

Inflation

All Capex and Opex inputs are in USD at start of forecast.

A yearly inflation rate can be input in the settings section to calculate Capex and Opex in the future during forecast calculations.

 

 

Net cash Flow calculation

When generating forecast, at every Ttmestep, Precept calculates:

 - Capex cost, taking into account inflation

 - Opex cost , taking into account inflation

 - Hydrocarbon sales, taking into account hydrocarbons price yearly inflation

 - Net Cash Flow = Hydrocarbon sales - CAPEX - OPEX
